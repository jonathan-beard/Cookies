\section*{Date Nut Drops}

\subsection*{Notes}
\textit{Yield: approximately 2 dozen cookies} \\
\textit{Oven: $300^\circ\mathrm{F}$ \quad Bake time: about $12$ minutes}

\vspace{1em}

%-------------------------------------------------
\subsection*{Original Recipe}

\subsubsection*{Ingredients}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\frac{2}{3}$ cup Crisco (about $11$ Tbsp)
  \item $1$ cup brown sugar
  \item $2$ eggs
  \item $2$ cups broken nuts
  \item $2$ cups seedless raisins
  \item $2$ cups chopped dates
  \item $\frac{3}{2}$ cups flour
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp baking powder
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp salt
  \item $\frac{1}{4}$ tsp cinnamon
  \item $\frac{1}{8}$ tsp cloves
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp allspice
  \item $2$ Tbsp orange \textit{or} pineapple juice
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Method}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Cream the Crisco and brown sugar together.
  \item Add eggs and cream well.
  \item Mix in nuts, raisins, and dates.
  \item Stir in the fruit juice.
  \item Add dry ingredients and mix until combined.
  \item Drop by teaspoon onto a greased baking sheet.
  \item Bake at $300^\circ\mathrm{F}$ for approximately $12$ minutes.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Variant: Rhubarb, Dried Cherry, and Walnut Drops}

\paragraph{Base Flavor Profile}
This variant replaces dates and raisins with tart fruit and structured nuts while preserving the original spice balance and low oven temperature.

%-------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{Version A: Fresh Rhubarb}

\paragraph{Ingredients}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\frac{2}{3}$ cup Crisco
  \item $1$ cup brown sugar
  \item $2$ eggs
  \item $2$ cups chopped walnuts
  \item $\frac{3}{2}$ cups dried cherries
  \item $\frac{3}{2}$ cups finely chopped fresh rhubarb
  \item $\frac{3}{2}$ cups flour
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp baking powder
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp salt
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp cinnamon
  \item $\frac{1}{4}$ tsp allspice
  \item $2$ Tbsp orange juice
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Method}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Cream the Crisco and brown sugar together.
  \item Add eggs and cream well.
  \item Fold in walnuts, dried cherries, and fresh rhubarb.
  \item Stir in the orange juice.
  \item Add dry ingredients and mix just until combined.
  \item Drop by teaspoon onto a greased baking sheet.
  \item Bake at $300^\circ\mathrm{F}$ for approximately $12$ minutes.
\end{enumerate}

%-------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{Version B: Frozen Rhubarb}

\paragraph{Special Preparation}
Frozen rhubarb must be thawed completely and drained well. After draining, gently press between towels to remove excess liquid.

\paragraph{Ingredients}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\frac{2}{3}$ cup Crisco
  \item $1$ cup brown sugar
  \item $2$ eggs
  \item $2$ cups chopped walnuts
  \item $\frac{3}{2}$ cups dried cherries
  \item $\frac{5}{4}$ cups thawed, well-drained frozen rhubarb
  \item $\frac{7}{4}$ cups flour
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp baking powder
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp salt
  \item $\frac{1}{2}$ tsp cinnamon
  \item $\frac{1}{4}$ tsp allspice
  \item $1$ Tbsp orange juice
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Method}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Cream the Crisco and brown sugar together.
  \item Add eggs and cream well.
  \item Fold in walnuts and dried cherries.
  \item Gently fold in drained rhubarb.
  \item Stir in orange juice.
  \item Add dry ingredients and mix carefully to avoid excess moisture release.
  \item Drop by teaspoon onto a greased baking sheet.
  \item Bake at $300^\circ\mathrm{F}$ for approximately $12$--$14$ minutes.
\end{enumerate}
